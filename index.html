--Epipelagic Zone (Sunlight Zone)--

Jellyfish, Sea turtle, Dolphin and Blue whale usually live in this Sunlight zone.
It cover roughly 5% of the ocean's average depth, it is the warmest of all layers, most visible light can penetrate this layer.
Due to the light and warmth, all sort of life live in this layer, such as seaweeds, coral reefs. This layer create around 50% of the atmospheric oxygen.

--Mesopelagic Zone (Twilight Zone)--

Crab, Octopus, Swordfish, Wolf ell and catshark live in this zone.
Only faint light reaches this zone, due to lack of light, living creature begin to glow. No plants grow within this layer, so creatures either feed by filtering the water or hunting other creature.
Human need to wear protection suits due to high pressure and lack of heat.

--Bathypelagic zone (Midnight Zone)--

Blobfish, Squid, Viperfish, Anglerfish and Titanic.
It makes up almost 90 percent of the ocean, it is called the midnight zone because of the midnight zone because of the complete absence of light. The only light found in this layer is obtained from animals’ bioluminescence, which they utilize in search of their food.
The bathypelagic zone temperature is constant around 4°C.

--Abyssopelagic zone (The abyss)--

Sea stars, shrimps and squids.
It is the pitch black bottom layer of the ocean as no sunlight reaches this layer. It is covered with thick layers of mud, sand and rocks made form dead plants and animals 'remains.
The water temperature is near freezing, only a small number of creature are found at such depths.

--Hadalpelagic Zone (the Trenches)--

Starfish, Liparid fish, Sea cucumber and Tube worms.
Deepest point at the bottom of Mariana Trench of the Pacific Ocean in Japan.
The temperature is marginally above freezing, and no natural light reaches this zone. The pressure is an incredible eight tons per square inch.
